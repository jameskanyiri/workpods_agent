# Statutory Deduction Rates

## NSSF (National Social Security Fund)

### Contribution Tiers

| Tier | Pensionable Earnings Range (KES) | Employee Rate | Employer Rate |
|------|----------------------------------|---------------|---------------|
| Tier I | First 7,000 | 6% | 6% |
| Tier II | 7,001 - 36,000 | 6% | 6% |

- Maximum employee contribution: KES 2,160/month (6% of 36,000)
- Maximum employer contribution: KES 2,160/month (matching)
- Total maximum: KES 4,320/month

### Calculation Example

For gross pay of KES 50,000:

```
Tier I:  7,000 x 6% = 420
Tier II: 29,000 x 6% = 1,740 (capped at 36,000 - 7,000 = 29,000)
Total Employee NSSF = 2,160
Total Employer NSSF = 2,160
```

## Housing Levy (Affordable Housing Levy)

| Party | Rate | Basis |
|-------|------|-------|
| Employee | 1.5% | Gross Pay |
| Employer | 1.5% | Gross Pay |

Total contribution: 3% of gross pay (shared equally).

## NITA (National Industrial Training Authority)

| Party | Amount | Basis |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Employer only | KES 50 | Per employee per month |

NITA is not deducted from employee pay. It is an employer-only obligation.

## Notes

- All rates are stored as database configuration and updated when legislation changes
- NSSF Tier II was introduced under the NSSF Act 2013; the transition schedule applies
- NHIF contributions are eligible for insurance relief (15% of premium, max KES 5,000/month)
